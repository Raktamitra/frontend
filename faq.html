Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Who can donate blood?

Most healthy adults between 18 and 65 years old and weighing more than 50 kg can donate blood, provided they meet eligibility criteria.

How often can I donate blood?

You can donate whole blood every 3 months. Men can donate every 12 weeks and women every 16 weeks.

Is blood donation safe?

Yes. Only sterile, single-use needles and equipment are used. Your safety is a top priority.

How long does blood donation take?

The actual donation takes about 8–10 minutes, but the total process takes around 30–45 minutes.

Can I donate if I have a tattoo or piercing?

Yes, but only if it was done more than 6 months ago using sterile equipment.

Will I feel weak after donating?

Most people feel normal. Some may feel a little tired, but rest and hydration help you recover quickly.

Can women donate during menstruation?

If the woman is feeling healthy and has good hemoglobin levels, it's usually safe. But it's best to avoid donating if feeling weak.

What are the eligibility requirements?

You must be healthy, weigh over 50 kg, and have a hemoglobin level above 12.5 g/dL.

Can I donate while on medication?

It depends on the medication. Many are acceptable, but some may temporarily disqualify you. Check with the medical team at the donation center.

Can I donate after COVID-19 or vaccination?

Yes, after 14 days of full recovery from COVID-19 or 1–7 days after vaccination, depending on the type.

Can diabetics donate blood?

Yes, if diabetes is well-controlled with diet or oral medication and the person is otherwise healthy.

Is summer or fasting season safe for donation?

Yes, as long as you are hydrated and have eaten before donating, it’s safe to donate even during these times.

What happens to my blood?

Your blood is tested, separated into components, and used for patients in need of transfusions in hospitals.

How much blood is collected?

About 350 to 450 ml, depending on your body weight and health condition.

Should I rest after donation?

Yes, rest for at least 10–15 minutes and stay hydrated. Avoid strenuous activity for the rest of the day.

Can I get infected from donating blood?

No. Only sterile, one-time-use needles are used, ensuring your complete safety.

Is blood donation paid?

No. In India and many other countries, blood donation is voluntary and unpaid to maintain ethical and safe practices.
